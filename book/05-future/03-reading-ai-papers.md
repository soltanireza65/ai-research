# Reading AI Research Papers

> **Status:** Stub chapter — Part II placeholder. Intended after Part I core modules are complete.

## Overview

Research papers are the primary channel for new AI ideas — but they are dense, notation-heavy, and assume familiarity with prior work. This chapter teaches a **repeatable reading workflow** for engineers who completed this handbook's math, PyTorch, neural network, and transformer modules and want to follow the field without drowning in jargon.

You will not learn to read papers by reading *about* reading papers alone. This stub outlines the structure; the full chapter will walk through annotated examples (attention, diffusion, RLHF) using skills you already built in Part I.

## Planned Topics

- Paper anatomy: abstract, introduction, related work, method, experiments, limitations
- First pass vs deep pass: when to skim and when to derive
- Tracing notation back to handbook chapters (gradients, attention, loss functions)
- Reproducing key figures and ablations in small PyTorch sandboxes
- Using arXiv, Papers With Code, and bibliographic chains
- Red flags: missing baselines, unclear evaluation, overclaimed generalization

## Prerequisites

Solid completion of Part I (`01-math` through `04-transformers`). Attempt at least one full paper — "Attention Is All You Need" — after Module 04.
